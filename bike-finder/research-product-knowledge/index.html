Product Knowledge

Bike Users

Purpose of the Bike

  • What do I want to do on my bicycle? Weekend riding? Commuting?
  • Where do I want to go? On-road? Off-road? City streets, canal paths or even the beach?
  • How much time/skill do I have looking after a bike?
  • What’s your plan for 6 to 12 months time? What potential do you want out of the bike?

Budget

  • How much are you willing to spend on a bike?
  • Complementary goods: Helmet, Gloves, Padded cycling shorts, Glasses, Bike specific shoes

Physical Product + Product Variations

Types and Styles

  • Mountain Bike: off road/trail riding. They generally have suspension either front or full and wide, grippy tyres
  • Hybrid Bike: built on a spectrum with some much closer to road bikes and others much closer to mountain bikes (can take on different situations) -> good for City riders, Commuting, People who take on a mix of terrain, Riding with the family, A runaround to take to the shops etc. (They are normally the cheapest type of bike), and Touring.
  • The Basics: quick progress on the roads with the least effort possible (road riding, touring ,communiting)
  • Electric: have an inbuilt motor which can be recharged to give the rider an extra boost (used in cities and will make hilly areas less daunting to inexperienced riders)
  • City: designed for short urban rides
  • Folding: designed for commuting and are often used by people travelling by train (ideal for short journeys)

Materials

  • Steel: Ideal for touring, Good for heavier riders as it is generally a very strong material
  • Aluminium: Less prone to rust making it a good touring or winter bike option. Stiffness means great power transfer through the frame. At the lower end of the price scale.
  • Carbon Fibre: Light but still incredibly strong. Aerodynamic frame shapes. A carbon frame can last a lifetime if well maintained.

Frame Size

Most bike manufacturers have size charts that list frame size based on height

Components + Specs

  • Gears: a low enough bottom gear to get you up the hills and a high enough top gear to allow you to reach the speeds you desire without your legs having to spin too fast or none
  • Brakes: rim brakes (traditional) and disc brakes (like motorcycle -> on road and mountain bikes)
  • Wheel: choice is largely a matter of performance nuances

Buying Experience

Local Bike Shops: close enough to you that you can use again and again for bikes, parts, repairs, clothing and even just advice

Pros

  • get face to face advice
  • can try and negotiate on the price (ex: get some accessories thrown in or a free service)
  • can be measured up and find a bike that fits
  • will often complete a basic bike setup like getting the saddle and handlebars in the right position
  • often they will offer a follow up service after a period of time. Cables stretch, brakes pads rub, spokes loosen. Bikes take a time to settle down so having someone around to do a routine follow up can be really useful

Cons

  • choice is significantly less than online
  • Price is often higher as shops have overheads such as rent and labour

Online: convenience of getting a bike to front door

Pros

  • can shop around multiple retailers to find the exact bike
  • Some bikes are exclusively available through online retailers
  • can often see reviews from other customers of the bikes

Cons

  • going to have to assemble the bike
  • have to set up saddle height and similar measurement-based aspects
  • unlikely to get any kind of service deal thrown in so this will be an additional cost in the first few months
  • won’t be able to have a test ride so size / fit may be an issue

Second Hand: can save a lot of money but you also run a much higher risk as it is highly unlikely to have any form of guarantee or returns policy

Pros

  • a much lower price -> saves a lot of money
  • likely to be less depreciation after purchase than on a new bike

Cons

  • don’t know entirely what you’re getting, especially if you haven’t viewed it beforehand
  • an take time to find the right bike in the right size
  • can end up with stolen or ‘fake’ bikes (scammed)
  • Sometimes the price may not lead to long term value
