Types and Styles

  • Mountain Bike: off road/trail riding. They generally have suspension either front or full and wide, grippy tyres
  • Hybrid Bike: built on a spectrum with some much closer to road bikes and others much closer to mountain bikes (can take on different situations) -> good for City riders, Commuting, People who take on a mix of terrain, Riding with the family, A runaround to take to the shops etc. (They are normally the cheapest type of bike), and Touring.
  • The Basics: quick progress on the roads with the least effort possible (road riding, touring ,communiting)
  • Electric: have an inbuilt motor which can be recharged to give the rider an extra boost (used in cities and will make hilly areas less daunting to inexperienced riders)
  • City: designed for short urban rides
  • Folding: designed for commuting and are often used by people travelling by train (ideal for short journeys)

Materials

  • Steel: Ideal for touring, Good for heavier riders as it is generally a very strong material
  • Aluminium: Less prone to rust making it a good touring or winter bike option. Stiffness means great power transfer through the frame. At the lower end of the price scale.
  • Carbon Fibre: Light but still incredibly strong. Aerodynamic frame shapes. A carbon frame can last a lifetime if well maintained.
